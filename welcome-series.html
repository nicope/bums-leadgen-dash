Key Findings & Optimisation Actions
📧 Email Flow
✅ Age-personalized Email 1 is exceptional
Baby/Toddler segments achieve 61% open rate and $2.40 RPR — well above industry benchmarks. The personalization strategy is working. Kids lag at 50% OR — worth testing new hero images or subject lines for the 6-10 age group.
⚠️ CBAB emails (2–4) are near-dead weight
Emails 2–4 generate $0.04–0.07 RPR vs $2.11 for Email 1 — a 97% drop. They still have 42–54% open rates, meaning audience is reading but not buying. The CBAB copy/offer isn't compelling enough. Consider replacing with social proof, UGC, or a product finder quiz.
⚠️ Email 3 open rate anomaly (54%)
Email 3 has a 54% open rate vs ~42% for Emails 2 and 4. This suggests the subject line is significantly better. Worth applying that subject line style to the other CBAB emails to lift overall engagement.
💡 Flash Sale emails (5–6) underperform
Despite urgency framing, Email 5 (Flash Sale 1) only generates $0.15 RPR. At step 5 the audience has already either converted or disengaged. Consider moving the flash sale to step 3, when audience is still warm.
✅ "Age not set" segment has highest CTR
Subscribers with no age data have 3.37% CTR and $2.47 RPR — higher than any named segment. Likely a motivated discovery audience. An onboarding quiz as part of the welcome flow could capture age data and make this the best-performing segment.
💬 SMS Flow
✅ SMS 1 is a top-performing revenue engine
SMS 1 generates $204k (76% of SMS flow revenue) with a 7.07% CTR and 1.61% CVR. A $1.63 RPR from a single text message is excellent. The 15OFF offer sent immediately after opt-in has peak buying intent.
⚠️ SMS 2 Contact Card has 4.81% unsub rate
SMS 2 generates 6,709 unsubscribes — nearly 2× the rate of other messages. This is the highest churn point in the entire welcome series. The contact card format may feel like spam or add friction. Consider replacing with a value-add message or moving it later in the sequence.
💡 SMS 3–5 are high-volume, low-return
SMS 3–5 combined reach 290k recipients but generate only $36k revenue ($0.12 RPR average). Meanwhile they accumulate 7,630 unsubscribes. Consider reducing to 2 follow-up SMS max, or switching to a different offer type (e.g. social proof, bestseller, urgency expiry).
✅ Hidden test: 10_off_75 had 16.2% CTR
The small test variant offering $10 off $75 (vs 15% off) showed 16.2% CTR and 3.02% CVR with a higher $129 AOV. Only 1,579 recipients so not conclusive — but this is worth a proper A/B test. Threshold-based offers may outperform percent discounts for SMS.
⚠️ Click rate decay (7% → ~3%) after SMS 1
Each subsequent SMS sees roughly the same CTR (~2.7–2.9%), meaning the urgency of the 15OFF offer is fully spent after message 1. Subsequent messages need a new hook — either a different offer, social proof angle, or product-specific message — to revive engagement.
